The Poincaré algebra

$$\begin{align} \left[ P_\mu,P_\nu \right] & = 0\\ \left[ M_{\mu\nu}, P_\rho \right] & = i\left(\eta_{\mu\rho}P_\nu - \eta_{\nu\rho}P_\mu\right)\\ \left[ M_{\mu\nu}, M_{\rho\sigma} \right] & = i\left( \eta_{\mu\rho} M_{\nu\sigma} - \eta_{\mu\sigma} M_{\nu\rho} - \eta_{\nu\rho} M_{\mu\sigma} + \eta_{\nu\sigma} M_{\mu\rho}\right) \end{align}$$

Casimir Operators

Casimir operators are ones that commute with all the generators of an algebra. The Poincaré algebra admits two Casimir operators \(P^2\) and \(W^2\). $$P^2 = P^\mu \eta_{\mu\nu} P^\nu\qquad W_\mu = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_{\rho\sigma\nu\mu}M^{\rho\sigma}P^\nu\qquad W^2 = -\frac{1}{4} J^{\rho\sigma}P^\nu J_{\gamma\delta}P_{\beta}\, \delta^{\beta\gamma\delta}_{\nu\rho\sigma}$$ We can easily verify the commutation relations $$\begin{align} \left[ P_\mu, P^2 \right] & = \eta^{\nu\rho}\left( \left[P_\mu,P_\nu\right]P_\rho + P_\nu\left[P_\mu,P_\rho\right] \right) = 0\\ \left[ M_{\mu\nu}, P^2 \right] & = \eta^{\rho\sigma}\left( \left[M_{\mu\nu},P_\rho\right]P_\sigma + P_\rho\left[M_{\mu\nu},P_\sigma\right] \right)\\ & = i\eta^{\rho\sigma}\left(\left( \eta_{\mu\rho}P_\nu - \eta_{\nu\rho}P_\nu \right)P_\sigma + P_\rho\left( \eta_{\mu\sigma}P_\nu - \eta_{\nu\sigma}P_\mu \right)\right)\\ & = i\left( P_\nu P_\mu - P_\mu P_\nu + P_\mu P_\nu - P_\nu P_\mu \right) = 0\\ & \\ \left[ P_\mu, W^2 \right] & = \frac{1}{4}\,\delta^{\beta\gamma\delta}_{\nu\rho\sigma}\left[ J^{\rho\sigma}P^\nu J_{\gamma\delta}P_{\beta}, P_\mu \right]\\ & = \frac{1}{4}\,\delta^{\beta\gamma\delta}_{\nu\rho\sigma}\left( J^{\rho\sigma}P^\nu\left[ J_{\gamma\delta},P_\mu \right] P_\beta + \left[ J^{\rho\sigma}, P_\mu \right] P^\nu J_{\gamma\delta} P_\beta \right)\\ & = \frac{i}{4}\,\delta^{\beta\gamma\delta}_{\nu\rho\sigma}\left( J^{\rho\sigma}P^\nu\left( \eta_{\gamma\mu} P_\delta - \eta_{\delta\mu} P_\gamma \right) P_\beta + \left( {\delta^\rho}_\mu P^\sigma - {\delta^\sigma}_\mu P^\rho \right) P^\nu J_{\gamma\delta} P_\beta \right)=0\\ \left[ M_{\mu\nu}, W^2 \right] & = \ldots \end{align}$$

\(\mathcal{N}=1\) Super-Poincaré algebra

$$\begin{align} \left[ P_\mu,P_\nu \right] & = 0\\ \left[ M_{\mu\nu}, P_\rho \right] & = i\left(\eta_{\mu\rho}P_\nu - \eta_{\nu\rho}P_\mu\right)\\ \left[ M_{\mu\nu}, M_{\rho\sigma} \right] & = i\left( \eta_{\mu\rho} M_{\nu\sigma} - \eta_{\mu\sigma} M_{\nu\rho} - \eta_{\nu\rho} M_{\mu\sigma} + \eta_{\nu\sigma} M_{\mu\rho}\right)\\ \{ Q_\alpha, \bar{Q}_{\dot{\beta}} \} & = 2\sigma_{\alpha \dot{\beta}}^\mu P_\mu\\ [Q_\alpha, M_{\mu\nu}] & = i{\left( \sigma_{\mu\nu} \right)_\alpha}^\beta Q_\beta\\ [\bar{Q}^\dot{\alpha}, M_{\mu\nu}] & = i{\left( \bar{\sigma}_{\mu\nu} \right)^\dot{\alpha}}_\dot{\beta} \bar{Q}^\dot{\beta} \end{align}$$ $$\sigma^{\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{4}\left(\sigma^\mu\bar{\sigma}^\nu - \sigma^\nu\bar{\sigma}^\mu\right)\qquad \bar{\sigma}^{\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{4}\left(\bar{\sigma}^\mu\sigma^\nu - \bar{\sigma}^\nu\sigma^\mu\right)$$

Conventions and notations

Here are some useful conventions when summing over \(SL(2,\mathbb{C})\) indices \(\alpha, \dot{\alpha}\) of Weyl spinors $$\begin{align} \psi\chi & = \psi^\alpha\chi_\alpha = \epsilon^{\alpha\beta}\psi_\beta \epsilon_{\alpha\gamma}\chi^{\gamma} = -\psi_\alpha\chi^\alpha = \chi^\alpha\psi_\alpha = \chi\psi\\ \bar{\psi}\bar{\chi} & = \bar{\psi}_{\dot{\alpha}}\bar{\chi}^{\dot{\alpha}} = \epsilon_{\dot{\alpha}\dot{\beta}}\bar{\psi}^{\dot{\beta}} \epsilon^{\dot{\alpha}\dot{\gamma}}\bar{\chi}_{\dot{\gamma}} = -\bar{\psi}^{\dot{\alpha}}\bar{\chi}_{\dot{\alpha}} = \bar{\chi}_{\dot{\alpha}}\bar{\psi}^{\dot{\alpha}} = \bar{\chi}\bar{\psi}\\ \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align} \left(\psi\chi\right)^* & = \bar{\psi}\bar{\chi}\\ \left(\psi\sigma^\mu\bar{\chi}\right)^* & = \chi\sigma^\mu\bar{\psi}\\ (\bar{\psi}\bar{\sigma}^\mu \chi )^* & = \bar{\chi}\bar{\sigma}^\mu \psi \end{align}$$

A simple Lagrangian

Consider a theory with a left handed Weyl fermion (\(\psi_\alpha\)) and a complex scalar field (\(\phi\)) whose Langrangian is $$\mathcal{L} = -\partial_\mu\phi^* \partial^\mu\phi - i\bar{\psi}\bar{\sigma}^\mu\partial_\mu\psi$$

Exercise 3.3.3 Show that the Lagrangian is invariant under the supersymmetry transformation \(\delta_\epsilon\phi = \sqrt{2}\epsilon \psi\quad \delta_\epsilon\psi_\alpha = \sqrt{2} i \left(\sigma^\mu\bar{\epsilon}\right)_\alpha\partial_\mu\phi\)

Writting out the transformations explicitly gives $$\begin{matrix} \delta_\epsilon\phi = \sqrt{2} \epsilon^\alpha \psi_\alpha & \delta_\epsilon \psi_\alpha = \sqrt{2} i \sigma_{\alpha\dot{\alpha}}^\mu \bar{\epsilon}^{\dot{\alpha}} \partial_\mu\phi\\ \delta_\epsilon\phi^* = \sqrt{2} \left(\epsilon^\alpha \psi_\alpha\right)^* = \sqrt{2} \bar{\epsilon}_{\dot{\alpha}} \bar{\psi}^{\dot{\alpha}} & \delta_\epsilon \bar{\psi}_{\dot{\alpha}} = - \sqrt{2} i \sigma_{\alpha\dot{\alpha}}^\mu \epsilon^{\alpha} \partial_\mu\phi^* \end{matrix}$$ We also note that the Lagrangian above gives the equations of motion $$\partial_\mu\partial^\mu \phi = 0\qquad \partial_\mu\partial^\mu \phi^* = 0\qquad \bar{\sigma}^{\dot{\alpha}\alpha\mu}\partial_\mu\psi_\alpha = 0\qquad \bar{\sigma}^{\dot{\alpha}\alpha\mu}\partial_\mu\bar{\psi}_{\dot{\alpha}} = 0$$ Hence, the variation the Lagrangian under this transformation is given by $$\begin{align} \delta_\epsilon \mathcal{L} & = -\partial_\mu\left(\delta_\epsilon\phi^*\right)\partial^\mu \phi - \partial_\mu\phi^*\partial^\mu\left(\delta_\epsilon\phi\right) - i\delta_\epsilon\bar{\psi}_{\dot{\alpha}}\bar{\sigma}^{\dot{\alpha}\alpha\mu}\partial_\mu\psi_\alpha - i\bar{\psi}_{\dot{\alpha}}\bar{\sigma}^{\dot{\alpha}\alpha\mu}\partial_\mu\left(\delta_\epsilon\psi_\alpha\right)\\ & = - \sqrt{2} \bar{\epsilon}_{\dot{\alpha}} \partial_\mu\bar{\psi}^{\dot{\alpha}}\partial^\mu \phi - \sqrt{2} \epsilon^\alpha\partial_\mu\phi^*\partial^\mu \psi_\alpha - \sqrt{2} \epsilon^{\alpha} \sigma_{\alpha\dot{\alpha}}^\mu \bar{\sigma}^{\dot{\alpha}\beta\nu} \partial_\mu\phi^* \partial_\nu\psi_\beta + \sqrt{2} \bar{\epsilon}^{\dot{\beta}} \bar{\psi}_{\dot{\alpha}}\bar{\sigma}^{\dot{\alpha}\alpha\mu} \sigma_{\alpha\dot{\beta}}^\nu \partial_\mu \partial_\nu\phi\\ & = 0 \quad [dF] \end{align}$$
