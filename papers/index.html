0. In essence

The non-ablian worlvolume action for a stack of Dp-branes is presented. The extra terms this action contains shows that Dp-branes can couple to RR-forms of all degrees (higher and lower). In the presence of background \(B\) or \(F\) a stack of separated D0-branes (\([\Phi^i,\Phi^j]=0\)) is unstable. The stable non-ablian configuration is equivalent to a bound state of N D0-branes and a spherical D2-brane. This effect show how a Dp-brane can puff up into a higher dimensional one.

1. Abelian Brane Worldvolume Action

The usual abelian world volume action for a single Dp-brane is takes the Born-Infeld form [8] $$S_{\text{BI}} = -T_p\int d^{p+1}d\xi\, e^{-\phi}\sqrt{-\det(P[G+B]_{ab}+2\pi l^2_s F_{ab})}$$ This action can be trusted at all order of \(\alpha^\prime\) for \(F\) but not for \(\partial F\) and other fields.
The couplings to RR forms is given by the Chern-Simons action $$S_{\text{CS}} = \mu_p\int P\left[\sum C^{(n)} \,e^B\right]e^{2\pi l^2_s F}$$ This coupling term shows that in the presence of a background Kalb-Ramond field, or world-volume gauge field, a Dp-brane might carry charges of lower RR forms. Such configurations have an interpretation in terms of bound states of D-branes of different dimensions [11].

2. Non-abelian Born-Infled Action

Generalising the above actions to a stack of Dp-branes is not as simple as introducing a trace of the integrant. Indeed, this can be seen by considering T-duality.
We start with the non-abelian action for the D9-brane, since it contains no scalar fields, we only need to include an overall trace $$S_{\text{BI}} = -T_9\int d^{10}\xi\, \text{Tr}\left(e^{-\phi}\sqrt{-\det(G_{ab}+B_{ab}+2\pi l^2_s F_{ab})}\right)$$ We then proceed to T-dualise the above on the directions \(i,j=p+1,\ldots,9\), for which the field transformation rules are $$E_{\mu\nu} = G_{\mu\nu}+B_{\mu\nu}$$ $$\tilde{E}_{ab}=E_{ab}-E_{ai}E^{ij}E_{jb},\quad \tilde{E}_{aj}=E_{ak}E^{kj},\quad \tilde{E}_{ij}=E^{ij}$$ $$e^{2\tilde{\phi}}=e^{2\phi}\det(E^{ij})$$ $$\tilde{C}^{(n)}_{\mu\cdots\nu\alpha y} = C^{(n-1)}_{\mu\cdots\nu\alpha} - (n-1)\frac{C^{(n-1)}_{[\mu\cdots\nu|y}G_{|\alpha]y}}{G_{yy}}$$ $$\tilde{C}^{(n)}_{\mu\cdots\nu\alpha\beta} = C^{(n+1)}_{\mu\cdots\nu\alpha\beta y} +n C^{(n-1)}_{[\mu\cdots\nu\alpha}B_{\beta]y} + n(n-1) \frac{C^{(n-1)}_{[\mu\cdots\nu|y}B_{|\alpha|y}G_{|\beta]y}}{G_{yy}}$$ $$\Phi^{p+1}\rightarrow A_{p+1},\quad A_p\rightarrow \Phi^p$$ Applying this T-duality to the D9-brane action results in $$\tilde{S}_{BI} = -T_p\int d^p\xi\,\text{STr}\left(e^{-\phi}\sqrt{\det\left( P[E_{ab}+E_{ai}{(Q^{-1}-\delta)^i}_b]+2\pi l^2_s F_{ab} \right))\det({Q^i}_j)}\right)$$ where $${Q^i}_j={\delta^i}_j+2i\pi l^2_s [\Phi^i,\Phi^k]E_{kj}$$ The symmetrised traced was introduced, without which the trace would not be well-defined. Unfortunately, this action does not seem to capture the full physics of the infrared limir [38]. However, with superpersymmetry, it does seem to describe the physics of the non-ablelian Yang-Mills fields [41].

3. Non-abelian Chern-Simons Term

The Chern-Simons action on the other hand generalises $$S_{\text{CS}} = \mu_p\int \text{Tr}\left( P \left[ e^{2\pi i l^2_s \text{i}_{\Phi} \text{i}_{\Phi}}\sum C^{(n)} e^B \right] e^{2\pi l^2_s F} \right)$$ Through the action of the interior products \(\text{i}_{\Phi}\), we see that Dp-branes can also couple to RR n-forms, with \(n=p+3,p+5,\ldots\) through the additional interactions involving commutators of the non-abelian scalars.

4. N D0-branes puff up into D2-brane

If we consider a stack of N D0-branes in a constant background RR 4-form field strength $$F^{(4)}_{tijk} = -2f\epsilon_{ijk},\quad i,j,k\in\{1,2,3\}$$ then the Born-Infeld potential yields a scalar potential of the form $$V(\Phi) = -\frac{\lambda^2 T_0}{4} \text{Tr}([\Phi^i,\Phi^j]^2)-\frac{i}{3}\lambda^2\mu_0\text{Tr}(\Phi^i\Phi^j\Phi^k)F^{(4)}_{tijk}(t)$$ A solution which minimises this potential is, in fact, not given by separated D0-branes by is instead given by $$\Phi^i = \frac{f}{2}\alpha^i$$ where \(\alpha^i\) are \(N\times N\) irreducible matrix representation of \(\mathfrak{su}(2)\). For this solution, the potential evaluates to $$V_N = -\frac{\pi^2l^3_sf^4}{6g}N(N^2-1)$$ The fact that this solution has lower potential energy than separated D0-branes indicates that the latter is unstable and will tend to condense out into this non-commutative solution. Geometrically, it corresponds to the non-commutative (or fuzzy) 2-sphere [53,54]. This solution can then be seen as a bound state of N D0-branes and a spherical D2-brane.
